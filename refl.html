Daniel's Recap

          Over the course of these few months, Bitcoin has been very volatile, going up and down and around. However, through these dips and peaks, one thing has not changed. The technology behind it has not changed. And so I still stand, knowing that this ambition will not go to waste.
          Our blockchain passion project has addressed and impacted this community in quite a way. The idea was to create a better understanding of what was behind Bitcoin, and not just to have mindless individuals go for a trend in hope of make a few quick bucks. The topic is very relevant to today’s world, for it may be our future currency. Adoption of it is already beginning, and even some governments are behind it now. By creating a better understanding, people would be able to be less ignorant and more creative, showing a new path for some. Many have learned, for we have had over dozens of people receive brochures, others talked to personally by us, and some who have visited our website. A few stories and comments are listed below. In the future, we hope the spread can go global.
          Through this project, we had many difficulties but were able to pull through and finish strong. Many abilities were gained from working on this project, providing a helpful push towards today’s reality. For one, my front end coding skills were practiced and developed, improving my HTML, CSS, Jquery, and JS. I was also able to learn how to incorporate various libraries, like hint.css or Scroll Reveal. Through all the talking, I learned how to communicate better, with a more systematic way of explaining information. Also, my eye for design has increased, learning about new ways to create websites and further improvements I can do. With this new knowledge, I was able to create a better and more interactive website. The pamphlet, the website, and the talking all were fruit from the tree of experience.
          Blockchain and bitcoin have been fascinating since I ever learned about them. So for the jist of it, we decided to work on it. I was able to teach Ian the main idea, and he was also able to introduce it to others. I had struggling times to fit in all my activities, so time management was a big achievement as well as persevering through bugs and issues. Although we were able to pull this off, I learned that I have much to know and little time to spare. The Passion Project was quite eye-opening, opening a new realm of opportunity.
